Your social media bio is your digital handshake. It's often the first — and sometimes only — thing people see before deciding whether to follow you, click your link, or scroll past. Here are 10 practical tips for creating a bio that converts views into followers.

## 1. Know Your Character Limits

Every platform has different character limits for bios:

| Platform | Bio Limit | Name Limit | Link Limit |
|----------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Instagram | 150 | 30 | 1 |
| TikTok | 80 | 24 | 1 |
| Discord | 190 | 32 | N/A |
| Twitter/X | 160 | 50 | 1 |

Write your bio in a text editor first to check the character count before pasting.

## 2. Use Symbols to Save Space

One well-placed symbol can replace multiple words:
- ❤️ instead of "I love"
- ✨ instead of "amazing" or "magic"
- → instead of "check out" or "click below"

## 3. Create Visual Structure with Dividers

Use decorative dividers to separate your bio into clear sections:
```
✨ Content Creator
─── ✦ ───
📧 hello@example.com
─── ✦ ───
↓ Latest Video ↓
```

## 4. Lead with Your Value Proposition

The first line of your bio should tell people what you do and why they should care. Skip the clichés and be specific:
- **Good**: "Teaching creators how to grow on TikTok"
- **Better**: "0 → 50K TikTok followers in 3 months. Here's how ↓"

## 5. Add a Clear Call-to-Action

Tell people exactly what to do next:
- "↓ New video every Friday"
- "DM for collabs"
- "Click the link for free resources"

## 6. Use Line Breaks Strategically

A wall of text is hard to read. Use line breaks to create breathing room between ideas. Most platforms support line breaks in bios — use them!

## 7. Include Searchable Keywords

Platforms like Instagram and TikTok use your bio text for search. Include relevant keywords naturally: "UX Designer | Figma tutorials | Design tips"

## 8. Match Your Bio to Your Content

Your bio should reflect what people will actually find on your profile. If you post gaming content, use gaming-related symbols and language. Consistency builds trust.

## 9. Update Regularly

Stale bios signal inactive accounts. Update your bio:
- When you hit a milestone
- When launching something new
- Seasonally (holiday themes, events)

## 10. Test, Test, Test

Write 3 versions of your bio and use each for 2 weeks. Track which gets more profile visits and link clicks. Data beats opinion every time.
