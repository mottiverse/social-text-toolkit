Kaomoji (顔文字) — literally "face characters" in Japanese — are expressive text-based emoticons that have become a global phenomenon. Unlike Western emoticons like `:)` which you read sideways, kaomoji are read straight on, creating expressive faces like (◕‿◕) and (╯°□°）╯︵ ┻━┻.

## The History of Kaomoji

Kaomoji emerged in Japan during the 1980s on early internet forums and bulletin boards. Japanese users wanted more expressive emoticons than the simple `:-)` style common in Western computing. By using the full range of Unicode characters — not just ASCII — Japanese typists could create faces with varied expressions, tears, sweat drops, and even blushing cheeks.

The term "kaomoji" combines:
- **顔 (kao)** — Face
- **文字 (moji)** — Character

## How to Use Kaomoji

Kaomoji work everywhere standard text works:
- **Social media bios**: Add personality to your Instagram or TikTok bio
- **Chat messages**: Express emotion in Discord, WhatsApp, or Messenger
- **Gaming**: Use kaomoji in game chat and usernames
- **Comments**: Stand out in YouTube or Reddit comments

Simply click to copy any kaomoji and paste it wherever you want. All kaomoji use standard Unicode characters, so they display correctly on virtually every platform.

## The Lenny Face Phenomenon

A special sub-category of kaomoji is the "Lenny Face" — `( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)` — which became famous on 4chan and Reddit for its suggestive, knowing expression. Lenny faces use combining diacritical marks to create the distinctive eyes and mouth, making them a technical achievement in Unicode art.
